# Brand guidelines

This page defines the cytario brand positioning, voice and tone, and visual identity principles. It is the authoritative reference for anyone producing cytario-branded materials -- from social media posts and website copy to trade show graphics and regulatory submissions.

---

## Brand positioning

cytario is a digital pathology and spatial biology analytics platform developed by Slash-m GmbH. The platform enables researchers and pathologists to work with spatial biology data -- transcripts, proteins, and cellular structures -- through an interoperable, open architecture.

**Category:** Digital pathology / spatial biology software (SaMD)

**Positioning statement:** "Your own Spatial Biology analytics platform."

**Tagline:** "Connecting Pathology"

The positioning emphasizes ownership and autonomy -- cytario is not a service but a platform that belongs to the institution using it. The tagline "Connecting Pathology" reflects the platform's role in bridging traditional pathology workflows with modern spatial biology analytics.

---

## Voice and tone

cytario communicates as a knowledgeable peer, not as a vendor. The audience consists of pathologists, research scientists, bioinformaticians, and laboratory directors -- professionals who value precision and evidence over marketing language.

### What to avoid

- Superlatives without evidence ("the best", "the most advanced", "revolutionary")
- Vague benefit claims ("transform your workflow", "unlock insights")
- Consumer marketing patterns (urgency, scarcity, emotional manipulation)
- Off-label claims -- any statement about diagnostic accuracy, clinical utility, or AI performance that is not backed by data submitted to a regulatory authority

## Visual identity principles

### Logo usage

The cytario logo exists in several approved variants (see Foundation > Logo for the full set). Choosing the right variant depends on context:

| Context | Recommended variant |
|---------|-------------------|
| Website header, light backgrounds | Full logo (standard colors) |
| Trade show materials, hero banners | Logo + "Connecting Pathology" slogan |
| App icon, favicon, small UI placements | Reduced logo (icon only) |
| Dark or brand-purple backgrounds | White or on-purple variant |
| Single-color printing, fax, regulatory stamps | Black monochrome variant |
| Branded accent on teal background | White + teal variant |

The **logo with slogan** ("Connecting Pathology") is the preferred variant for first-impression touchpoints: trade show walls, business cards, letterheads, and presentation title slides. Use the standalone logo when the tagline would be too small to read or when space is constrained.

### The cell/molecule brand pattern

The cytario visual identity includes a distinctive **cell/molecule pattern** -- an organic, repeating motif of interconnected circular shapes in purple and teal. This pattern was designed by covermade and is a core brand element.

### Color usage rules

The brand palette is built on two primary colors -- **purple** (`#5c2483`) and **teal** (`#35b7b8`) -- with a neutral gray scale. See Foundation > Colors for the full system.

Key rules:

- **Purple is the dominant brand color.** It carries authority and is used for headings, brand surfaces, and secondary buttons.
- **Teal is the accent color.** It provides contrast and energy. Use it for primary actions, interactive highlights, and decorative elements.
- **Never use teal-500 for body text on white backgrounds.** It fails WCAG AA contrast. Use teal-700 (`#217d7e`) or darker for text.
- **Regulatory submissions** should use the monochrome (black) logo and avoid color where it could be misinterpreted as decorative emphasis on specific claims.

### Typography

cytario uses **Montserrat** as the sole typeface across all materials. See Foundation > Typography for the full scale.

- Do not substitute other typefaces, including system defaults, in branded materials.
- When Montserrat is not available (plain-text emails, terminal output), fall back to the system sans-serif.
