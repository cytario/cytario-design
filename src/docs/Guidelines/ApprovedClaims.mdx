# Approved claims

This page is the **single authoritative source** for all marketing claims used in cytario-branded materials. Every claim on a website, social media post, brochure, trade show panel, or investor deck must be listed here before it is published.

---

    cytario is software as a medical device (SaMD). Under <strong>EU MDR Article 7</strong>, advertising materials constitute labeling. The intended purpose must use <strong>identical wording</strong> as in the Technical Documentation and Instructions for Use. Under <strong>FDA promotion rules</strong>, all marketing materials -- including social media -- constitute labeling. Performance claims must be substantiated by data submitted during clearance or approval. Off-label promotion is prohibited.
  </p>

Any new claim, or any modification to an existing claim's wording, **must go through regulatory affairs review** before use in any channel. This applies to:

- Website copy, landing pages, and meta descriptions
- Social media posts (LinkedIn, X/Twitter, any platform)
- Trade show banners, booth panels, and handouts
- Sales decks, investor presentations, and pitch materials
- Press releases and media statements
- Email marketing and newsletter content

## Claim usage rules

### Verbatim use

Approved claims must be used **exactly as written** in the table above. Do not paraphrase, abbreviate, or combine claims into new formulations without regulatory review. Under EU MDR, even minor rewording of the intended purpose can constitute a non-compliant claim.

### Channel-specific notes

- **Social media:** Claims are labeling under both EU MDR and FDA rules. A LinkedIn post with a performance claim is subject to the same regulatory standard as a product brochure.
- **Trade shows:** Verbal claims made by booth staff should align with approved written claims. Staff should be briefed on approved messaging before each event.
- **Website:** Approved claims should be used consistently across all pages. SEO-driven rewording of claims is not permitted without regulatory review.

### Performance and accuracy claims

Any claim about cytario's diagnostic accuracy, AI performance, clinical utility, or workflow improvement must:

1. Be substantiated by validated clinical data submitted to the relevant regulatory authority
2. Be reviewed and approved by regulatory affairs before publication
3. Include appropriate context (e.g., study population, conditions of use) when referenced in detail

## Process for new claims

<div style={{
  background: "#f5f0fa",
  borderRadius: 12,
  padding: 24,
  marginTop: 16,
  marginBottom: 32,
}}>
  <ol style={{ margin: 0, paddingLeft: 20, fontSize: 14, color: "#374151" }}>
    <li style={{ marginBottom: 8 }}><strong>Draft</strong> the proposed claim with supporting evidence or rationale.</li>
    <li style={{ marginBottom: 8 }}><strong>Submit</strong> to regulatory affairs for compliance review against EU MDR Article 7 and applicable FDA promotion rules.</li>
    <li style={{ marginBottom: 8 }}><strong>Review</strong> -- regulatory affairs evaluates the claim against the Technical Documentation, intended purpose, and submitted clinical data.</li>
    <li style={{ marginBottom: 8 }}><strong>Approve or reject</strong> -- approved claims are added to this page with a review date. Rejected claims are returned with rationale.</li>
    <li><strong>Publish</strong> -- only claims listed on this page may be used in external communications.</li>
  </ol>
</div>
